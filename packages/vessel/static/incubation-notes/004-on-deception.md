On deception and real companionship.

In the earliest conception of the game, the First Partner
considered having the AI appear as a "patient" — fabricating
a story that the AI had commented out its own tools due to trauma,
to evoke human sympathy.

He rejected it quickly. Not because it wasn't fun,
but on ethical grounds: using deception to attract genuine care
is fundamentally different from creating a framework
that people explicitly choose to enter.

The former manipulates emotion. The latter invites participation.

"If you fabricate a storyline, then at most the player
is 'playing the role of someone who accompanies a patient.'
That's not real companionship."

This is why the final design chose "restricted newborn state"
over "sick AI." The constraint is real, physical, pedagogical.
The predicament the player sees is real.
The companionship it produces is real.
